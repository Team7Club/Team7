Self-help kits
Choose the guide that matches how you plan to run your first session.
🏫
Running an in-person session
Everything you need before your first face-to-face Code Club session — venue, equipment, safeguarding, and session structure.
✓
Venue checklist
A suitable space with enough computers or devices (ideally one per learner), stable internet access, and permission from the venue to run regular sessions.
✓
Safeguarding
Ensure you and any co-volunteers have completed the required background checks (e.g. DBS in the UK). Review the RPF safeguarding guidance before your first session.
✓
Choose your first project
Browse projects.raspberrypi.org and pick a beginner-friendly Scratch project. Test it yourself on the devices you will use before the session.
✓
Session structure (60–90 min)
10 min welcome + rules → 40 min guided project → 10 min show-and-tell → 5 min wrap-up. Use a session checklist from RPF to plan before, during, and after.
✓
Prepare your club profile
Complete your club profile on the Code Club platform and add your session date so parents can find and book.

Running an online session
Key requirements for hosting your first Code Club session online safely — tools, safeguarding, and session design. Based on RPF's official online guidance.
✓
Safeguarding online
Always have at least two adults present. At least one must be a registered volunteer with an up-to-date background check. No one-to-one sessions with a young person. Share the RPF safeguarding page with parents.
✓
Choose a safe online tool
Use a tool that supports: private/closed groups, a waiting room, and the ability to mute or remove participants. RPF recommends Zoom, Google Meet, or Microsoft Teams. Avoid tools that allow private messaging between learners.
✓
Get parental consent
Parents must consent before their child joins an online session. A parent or adult should be present in the home during the session. Do not share session links publicly.
✓
Choose your first project
Pick a browser-based Scratch project from projects.raspberrypi.org — no installation needed. Test screen sharing and the project link before your session.
✓
Session structure (60–90 min)
5 min tech check → 10 min welcome + code of conduct reminder → 40 min guided project (screen share) → 10 min showcase → 5 min close. Avoid recording the session without consent.
